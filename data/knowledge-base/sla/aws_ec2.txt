SERVICE: Amazon EC2 (Elastic Compute Cloud)
TYPE: COMPUTE

UPTIME GUARANTEE:

Region-Level SLA: 99.99% monthly uptime when instances are deployed across ≥2 Availability Zones (AZs) or ≥2 regions
Instance-Level SLA: 99.5% monthly uptime per individual EC2 instance
Automatic hourly credit: No charge for any instance unavailable for >6 minutes in a clock hour

FAILURE CONDITIONS:

Instance-Level: Instance has no external network connectivity
Region-Level: All instances across ≥2 AZs (or cross-region if single AZ) simultaneously have no external connectivity
Downtime measured as percentage of unavailable minutes in a monthly billing cycle

SERVICE CREDITS:

Region-Level SLA:

<99.99% and ≥99.0% → 10% credit
<99.0% and ≥95.0% → 30% credit
<95.0% → 100% credit

Instance-Level SLA:

<99.5% and ≥99.0% → 10% credit
<99.0% and ≥95.0% → 30% credit
<95.0% → 100% credit
Credits apply only to future EC2 payments
No refunds; minimum credit must exceed $1
Claims must be submitted within 2 billing cycles
Region-Level and Instance-Level claims cannot be combined

IMPORTANT RULES:

SLA applies per account and per region or per instance
Multi-AZ deployment is required for higher (99.99%) SLA coverage
Must provide logs, timestamps, and resource IDs to claim credits
Credits are the sole remedy for service failure
Excludes one-time payments (e.g., Reserved Instances upfront costs)
AWS validation of outage is mandatory

Exclusions:

Force majeure events
Internet/network issues outside AWS boundary
Customer misconfiguration or failure to respond to recovery events
Customer-owned hardware/software failures
Account suspension or termination by AWS

ARCHITECTURE BEST PRACTICES:

Deploy workloads across multiple AZs to qualify for Region-Level SLA
Use multi-region architecture for critical workloads
Implement health checks and auto-recovery mechanisms
Enable logging and monitoring for SLA claim validation
Avoid single-instance architectures for production systems
Design for fault tolerance and failover

SECURITY / COMPLIANCE NOTES:

Customer must maintain logs for SLA verification
Sensitive data in logs must be redacted before submission
Responsibility for configuration and availability monitoring lies with customer (shared responsibility model)

RISK IMPLICATION:

Single-instance deployments have lower availability guarantee (99.5%)
Misconfiguration or lack of redundancy leads to no SLA protection
External dependency failures (e.g., ISP issues) are not covered
Financial compensation is limited to service credits only (no direct refunds)

ACTION IMPACT:

TERMINATE → Loss of SLA coverage and potential downtime impact
DOWNSIZE → May reduce redundancy, increasing risk of SLA violation
SECURE → Improves resilience against misconfiguration-related exclusions
SCALE → Horizontal scaling across AZs increases availability and SLA compliance
