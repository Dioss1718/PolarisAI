SERVICE: Microsoft Online Services (General SLA Framework)
TYPE: MULTI_SERVICE

UPTIME GUARANTEE:

Service Levels defined per individual service (not global)
Uptime measured over:
30-day Applicable Period (Azure Pay-As-You-Go services)
Monthly billing cycle (other services)
Uptime calculated based on Downtime excluding Scheduled Downtime

FAILURE CONDITIONS:

Downtime defined per service (e.g., login failure, API failure, connectivity loss)
Incident = single or multiple events causing service unavailability
Failure includes:
No external connectivity
API failures (Error Codes)
Authentication or operation failures
Scheduled Downtime is excluded from failure calculation

SERVICE CREDITS:

Credits issued as % of Applicable Service Fees
Max credit ≤ total monthly service fees for affected service
No compensation for indirect losses (e.g., revenue loss)
Only one Service Credit per service per Applicable Period

General Credit Pattern (varies by service):

Minor SLA breach → ~10%
Moderate breach → ~25–50%
Severe breach → up to 100%

IMPORTANT RULES:

SLA applies only to paid services (free tiers and previews excluded)
Claims must include:
Incident description
timestamps and duration
affected resources
logs and error details
Azure claims: must be submitted within 60 days
Other services: claim within next billing cycle
Microsoft validates claims and processes within ~45 days
Credits are the sole remedy for SLA violations

Exclusions:

Force majeure events (natural disasters, war, etc.)
External network or device failures
Third-party software/services issues
Customer misconfiguration or security failures
Unsupported configurations or exceeding quotas
Scheduled maintenance windows
Performance degradation without full outage
User-initiated operations (restart, scale, failover)

ARCHITECTURE BEST PRACTICES:

Design geo-redundant and multi-region architectures
Avoid dependency on single datacenter deployments
Implement monitoring and logging (Azure Monitor recommended)
Use supported configurations and follow service guidelines
Avoid exceeding quotas and throttling limits
Implement retry and fault-tolerant patterns
Design for failover and resilience

SECURITY / COMPLIANCE NOTES:

Customer responsible for security configuration and access control
Logs required for SLA claims must be provided and validated
Shared responsibility model applies across all services
Unauthorized access due to weak security practices invalidates SLA claims

RISK IMPLICATION:

Single-region or non-resilient deployments are not protected
Misconfiguration or unsupported usage voids SLA coverage
No financial compensation beyond service credits
Performance issues without full outages are not covered
Dependency on third-party or external networks increases risk exposure

ACTION IMPACT:

TERMINATE → Loss of SLA coverage and service continuity
DOWNSIZE → Increased risk of throttling, performance issues, SLA breach
SECURE → Reduces risk of exclusion due to unauthorized access or misconfiguration
SCALE → Improves resilience, availability, and SLA compliance
