SERVICE: Amazon RDS (Relational Database Service)
TYPE: DATABASE

UPTIME GUARANTEE:

Multi-AZ DB Instance / Multi-AZ DB Cluster: ≥99.95% monthly uptime
Single-DB Instance: ≥99.5% monthly uptime
Uptime measured per 1-minute intervals over monthly billing cycle

FAILURE CONDITIONS:

Service considered unavailable when all connection requests fail during a 1-minute interval
Applies to:
Multi-AZ DB Instance
Multi-AZ DB Cluster
Single-DB Instance
Downtime calculated as percentage of failed connection intervals

SERVICE CREDITS:

Multi-AZ SLA:

<99.95% and ≥99.0% → 10% credit
<99.0% and ≥95.0% → 25% credit
<95.0% → 100% credit

Single-DB SLA:

<99.5% and ≥99.0% → 10% credit
<99.0% and ≥95.0% → 25% credit
<95.0% → 100% credit
Credits apply only to future RDS payments
No refunds; minimum credit must exceed $1
Claims must be submitted within 2 billing cycles
Multi-AZ and Single-DB claims cannot be combined

IMPORTANT RULES:

SLA applies per DB instance or cluster and per region
Multi-AZ deployment required for higher availability guarantee (99.95%)
Must submit logs, timestamps, DB instance IDs, and regions for claims
Credits are the sole remedy for service failures
AWS validation required for claim approval

Exclusions:

Force majeure events
Network/internet issues outside AWS boundary
Customer actions or misconfigurations
Use of Micro DB instance class or similar limited-resource instances
Not following operational best practices (e.g., overloading DB, excessive tables)
Database engine failures causing crashes
Insufficient I/O capacity causing long recovery times
Customer-owned infrastructure or software issues
Account suspension or termination by AWS

ARCHITECTURE BEST PRACTICES:

Use Multi-AZ deployments for production workloads
Avoid Single-DB instances for high availability systems
Provision adequate IOPS and storage for workload demands
Implement connection retry and failover logic
Monitor database health and performance metrics
Follow RDS operational guidelines to avoid exclusion conditions
Avoid resource overloading and excessive schema complexity

SECURITY / COMPLIANCE NOTES:

Logs required for SLA claims must redact sensitive data
Customer responsible for proper database configuration and monitoring
Shared responsibility model applies to database performance and availability

RISK IMPLICATION:

Single-DB deployments have lower availability and higher downtime risk
Misconfiguration or poor capacity planning voids SLA eligibility
Engine-level failures and performance bottlenecks are not covered
Financial compensation limited to service credits only

ACTION IMPACT:

TERMINATE → Loss of database availability and SLA coverage
DOWNSIZE → Increased risk of performance degradation and SLA violation
SECURE → Reduces risk of misconfiguration-related exclusions
SCALE → Improves availability and performance, supports SLA compliance
